Edmonton operates a three-stream waste collection system, and Royalwood residents have access to all three curbside carts. Using each stream correctly keeps useful materials out of landfill and supports Edmonton's waste diversion goals.

## The Three Streams

### Green Cart — Organics

The green cart accepts:
- Food scraps (cooked and raw, including meat and dairy)
- Food-soiled paper (napkins, paper towels, pizza boxes)
- Yard waste (grass clippings, leaves, small branches up to 3cm diameter)
- Pet waste (bagged in certified compostable bags)

**Not accepted:** Plastic bags (even "compostable" ones not certified to AS 4736), liquids, large branches

Organics are processed at the Edmonton Composting Facility and turned into nutrient-rich compost. The resulting product is sold as GreenPower compost.

### Blue Cart — Recycling

The blue cart accepts:
- Paper and cardboard (flattened)
- Plastics #1–7 (rigid plastics, cleaned)
- Glass bottles and jars
- Metal cans (rinsed)

**Not accepted:** Plastic bags, styrofoam, food-soiled items, electronics, batteries

### Black Cart — Landfill

Only items that cannot go in the green or blue cart should go here. The goal is to minimize what ends up in the black cart.

## Home Composting

Home composting complements the green cart and can handle garden waste year-round. The City of Edmonton offers:

- **Subsidized home composter sales** — check [edmonton.ca/composters](https://www.edmonton.ca/waste) for availability
- **Free composting workshops** — offered through Edmonton Public Library and community leagues

## Electronics & Hazardous Waste

These items should **never** go in any curbside cart:

- Batteries, paint, chemicals, medications → **Eco Stations**
- Electronics and small appliances → **Eco Stations** or retailer take-back programs
- Fluorescent bulbs → **Eco Stations**

Nearest Eco Station: **5205 – 59 Avenue NW** (check edmonton.ca for seasonal hours)

## Reduce & Reuse First

The most effective waste action is producing less in the first place:
- Buy in bulk and avoid single-use packaging where possible
- Use the [Edmonton Tool Library](https://www.edmontontoollibrary.ca) for infrequent-use tools instead of buying new
- Donate usable items to thrift stores or use neighbourhood buy-nothing groups
