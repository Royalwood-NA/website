One of Royalwood's most valued features is its abundance of green space — from maintained parks to the natural vegetation around the storm pond system.

## Royalwood Park (Central Greenspace)

The central park is home to:
- A playground structure (suitable for ages 2–12)
- Picnic tables and a gazebo
- Open grass area used for informal sports and community events
- Benches along the perimeter path

The RNA hosts its annual BBQ and fall cleanup in and around this space.

## Storm Pond & Naturalized Area

The storm pond is a functioning part of the City's stormwater management system, but it also serves as a naturalized green corridor. You'll often see:

- Canada geese and mallards (especially in spring and fall)
- Songbirds nesting in the willows and shrubs
- Residents walking dogs and enjoying the loop path

**Please do not feed waterfowl.** It disrupts natural feeding behaviour and can lead to water quality issues.

## Pathway Network

Royalwood has an extensive paved pathway system that connects to:
- The Millwoods Trail network
- Mill Creek Ravine (via connector paths to the north)

Pathways are maintained by the City of Edmonton. To report damage or debris, use the **311 app** or call 311.

## Winter Use

In winter, the pathway network is generally cleared within 48 hours of snowfall events. The storm pond area is not maintained for skating — ice conditions are unpredictable and unsupervised skating is not recommended.

## Reporting Issues

For park maintenance concerns (broken equipment, graffiti, fallen trees), contact **City of Edmonton 311**. For issues specific to RNA-maintained areas, contact [info@royalwoodna.org](mailto:info@royalwoodna.org).
