Royalwood is served by Edmonton Transit Service (ETS). While the neighbourhood is primarily car-oriented, transit options connect residents to the broader city network.

## Bus Routes

Check the [ETS website](https://www.edmonton.ca/transit) or the **Edmonton Transit** app for current routes, schedules, and real-time arrivals. Routes and numbers change periodically.

Key connections from the Royalwood / Millwoods area:
- Routes running along 66 Street NW and 23 Avenue NW serve as main corridors
- Express and community bus routes connect to **Millwoods Transit Centre**

## Millwoods Transit Centre

The Millwoods Transit Centre (MMWTC) is the main hub for southeast Edmonton. From here you can connect to:
- LRT at **Millwoods Station** (Valley Line West extension)
- Downtown and university express routes
- Cross-city connector routes

## LRT Access

The **Valley Line LRT** provides a direct, traffic-free connection from Millwoods into downtown Edmonton. Journey time to City Hall LRT Station is approximately 30–35 minutes.

Plan your trip using the [ETS Trip Planner](https://www.edmonton.ca/transit/tripplanner).

## Cycling & Active Transit

Several primary pathways connect Royalwood to the broader Millwoods trail network, which in turn connects to the Mill Creek Ravine multi-use trail system. The City of Edmonton's cycling map is available at [edmonton.ca/cycling](https://www.edmonton.ca/transportation/cycling).

## Accessibility

All ETS buses are low-floor accessible. DATS (Disabled Adult Transit Service) provides door-to-door accessible transit for eligible Edmonton residents. Learn more at [edmonton.ca/DATS](https://www.edmonton.ca/transit/DATS).
