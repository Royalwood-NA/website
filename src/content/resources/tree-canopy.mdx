Trees are one of Royalwood's most important community assets. A healthy urban tree canopy provides shade, reduces stormwater runoff, supports wildlife, and improves air quality. Protecting and growing it is a shared responsibility.

## The City's Urban Forest Management Plan

Edmonton manages approximately 400,000 trees on public land. The City's [Urban Forest Management Plan](https://www.edmonton.ca/urbanforest) outlines goals for canopy coverage, species diversity, and long-term stewardship.

Key commitments:
- Planting 100,000 trees over 10 years
- Diversifying species to improve resilience against pests and climate change
- Prioritizing neighbourhood equity in canopy coverage

## Trees on Public Property

Trees in boulevards, parks, and public spaces are maintained by the City of Edmonton. Do not prune, top, or remove these trees without authorization.

**To report a damaged, diseased, or hazardous public tree:**  
Use the 311 app or call 311. Include the location and a photo if possible.

**To request a new tree on your boulevard:**  
Apply through the City's [Free Trees for Edmonton program](https://www.edmonton.ca/trees). Space and species availability vary by location.

## Trees on Private Property

You are responsible for trees on your own property. Some considerations:

- Branches overhanging a neighbour's property can legally be trimmed to the property line, but the trunk owner is generally responsible for the tree's health
- Removal of healthy mature trees is discouraged and may require notification to the City in some circumstances
- Contact a certified arborist for any significant tree work — improper pruning can cause long-term damage

## Dutch Elm Disease

Alberta remains one of the last elm-free regions in North America due to strict provincial controls. It is **illegal to prune or move elm material** between April 1 and July 31 (the elm bark beetle flight season).

Report suspected Dutch Elm Disease at: [dutchelm.alberta.ca](https://www.alberta.ca/dutch-elm-disease)

## RNA Tree Initiatives

The RNA monitors our neighbourhood's tree canopy condition as part of our environmental stewardship work. If you have concerns about trees near you, email [environment@royalwoodna.org](mailto:environment@royalwoodna.org).
