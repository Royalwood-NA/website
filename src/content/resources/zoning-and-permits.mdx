Most of Royalwood is designated as low-density residential under the City of Edmonton Zoning Bylaw. Understanding your zoning can help you plan renovations, additions, or outbuildings and avoid costly surprises.

## Royalwood Zoning

Most single-detached homes in Royalwood fall under:
- **RSL (Residential Small Lot)** — standard single-detached residential
- **RF1 (Single Detached Residential)** — in some older portions of the neighbourhood

Newer multi-family areas may include **RM** (Medium Density Residential) designations.

To confirm your property's zoning, use the City's [Zoning Map](https://maps.edmonton.ca/map.aspx).

## When You Need a Permit

A development permit is typically required for:
- New residential construction
- Additions to an existing home (decks over 0.6m, sunrooms, garages)
- Secondary suites (basement or garage)
- Significant exterior changes
- Demolition

A building permit is required for structural work, electrical, plumbing, and mechanical changes.

**When in doubt, call 311 or visit the City's [permit information page](https://www.edmonton.ca/permits).**

## How to Apply

Permits are applied for through the City of Edmonton's **Self-Service Portal**:  
[epcor.com permits portal → via edmonton.ca](https://www.edmonton.ca/permits)

Processing times vary by permit type. Simple residential permits are often processed within 10–20 business days.

## Secondary Suites

Secondary suites (basement or garage suites) are permitted in Royalwood subject to zoning regulations and a development permit. The City has a dedicated guide:  
[edmonton.ca/secondary-suites](https://www.edmonton.ca/building_neighbourhoods/secondary-suites)
